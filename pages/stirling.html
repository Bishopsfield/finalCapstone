The Short Stirling was a British four-engined heavy bomber of the Second World War. It was the first four-engined bomber to be used by the RAF and was designed and built by Short Brothers. The Stirling was larger and heavier than the RAF's previous three-engined bombers, the Hampden and the Wellington, and was able to carry a heavier bomb load for a longer range. However, it had a relatively low operational ceiling and was vulnerable to enemy fighter attacks. Despite these limitations, the Stirling played a vital role in the RAF's strategic bombing campaign and was used in a variety of roles, including transport and special operations. The Stirling was phased out of service in 1943, but it remains an important part of British aviation history.
